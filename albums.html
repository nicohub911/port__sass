Stuck in the Sound

"Stuck in the Sound" is the band's first "album" (compact disc), produced by their own record label Stuck Records, consisting of 10 songs: Delicious Dog, Tears in the Wall, Pretty Temple, You're Special, Fading Star, I Shot My Friend, No Comment, A Roundabout Song, Space Champagne, and On a Velvet Ground.

Shoegazing Kids

"Shoegazing Kids" is the second studio album by French indie rock band Stuck in the Sound. Released on 26 January 2009 through Discograph Records, the album was available as both a physical release and download in their home country of France, but exclusively as a download in a number of other countries. The songs on the album were all recorded in the same studio and the record was mixed in New York City by producer Nick Sansano and mastered by Ted Jensen. The record contains a mixture of indie rock and shoegaze genres, and the track "Ouais" was in particular hailed for its Britpop style.

Pursuit

"Pursuit" is the third studio album by the French indie rock band Stuck in the Sound, released on January 30, 2012. The album marked a shift in the band's sound, introducing more solid rock elements in contrast to their earlier, more indie style. The lead single, "Brother," gained notable popularity, and the album as a whole was well-received, solidifying Stuck in the Sound's position in the French and international music scene.

Survivor

"Survivor" is the fourth studio album by the French indie rock band Stuck in the Sound, released on March 11, 2016. The album showcased a further evolution in the band's sound, leaning more towards pop rock while retaining their indie rock roots. Singles such as "Pop Pop Pop" and "Miracle" were released prior to the album, and '''Survivor''' was generally well-received for its catchy melodies and polished production, marking another step in the band's career.

Billy Believe

"Billy Believe" is the fifth studio album by the French indie rock band Stuck in the Sound, released on March 1, 2019. This album saw the band continue to explore a more accessible sound, blending their indie rock foundation with elements of pop and electronic music. Tracks like "Alright" showcased this direction, gaining significant attention. '''Billy Believe''' presented a further evolution for Stuck in the Sound, demonstrating their willingness to experiment with different sonic textures while maintaining their characteristic energy.

Live in Mexico City

"Live in Mexico City" is a live EP by the French indie rock band Stuck in the Sound, released on March 15, 2024. This EP captures a selection of live performances recorded at Bajo Circuito in Mexico City. It features live renditions of popular tracks such as "Sonora," "Let's Go," "Riots," "Shoot Shoot," and "Pop Pop Pop." '''Live in Mexico City''' serves as a testament to the band's energetic live shows and their connection with their Mexican fanbase. The EP was later released on vinyl in December 2024.
